I am passionate about leveraging GIS technology to address pressing environmental challenges, with a particular focus on advancing renewable energy solutions. My research centers on applying GIS to identify optimal locations for renewable energy projects, such as solar farms and wind turbines, by integrating spatial data, environmental factors, and socio-economic considerations. By analyzing land use patterns, resource availability, and environmental sensitivities, I aim to develop data-driven approaches that minimize ecological impacts while maximizing energy efficiency. Through this work, I seek to empower decision-makers with actionable insights that facilitate the transition to cleaner energy systems. I believe GIS has the potential to bridge the gap between science and policy, enabling more informed decisions that align with global sustainability goals. By contributing to renewable energy initiatives, I aim to combat climate change, promote resource stewardship, and support the development of resilient communities, fostering a sustainable future for generations to come.

Projects

This project utilized GIS tools to identify potential sites for solar-powered irrigation projects. I conducted site suitability assessments, analyzing factors like terrain, water sources, and accessibility. I compiled detailed feasibility reports on each site, evaluating their potential for successful implementation. Community outreach was also a key part of the project, ensuring local stakeholders were informed and engaged. The goal was to identify sustainable, viable locations for irrigation solutions using renewable energy.
